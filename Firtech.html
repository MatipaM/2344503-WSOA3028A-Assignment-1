Firtech Internship

This past year, I have been interning at Firtech, a software development/automation company in South Africa. I am currently working as the only front end developer, building the front pages for an unreleased document storage application. The app's purpose is to assist in the safe storage of a user's documents both online and physically. If the user chooses to upgrade their subscription they have the additional option of having documents certified, and/or destroyed for them at the press of a button.

The web application, DocuVault was built in Blazor. Initially I had struggled a lot working with Blazor as I had limited experience as both an app developer and a web developer. Most of my development experience had come from my game design courses. However, I found that through multitudes of on-going iterations my skills have improved quite drastically over the past two to three months I have been working on this project. I also realised that it was much easier to fine tune smaller issues when I had built the entire page and was just going back and refining the details.

Logo Design

When designing the DocuVault logo, I knew from the start I wanted it to be quite dependent on visual communication. My first decision was whether to incorporate the image of a vault or that of a lock. A vault made more sense because its in the name, but a lock was simpler and therefore easier for users to recognise. I decided to go with the vault and then tried to highlight a D and V shape from that image.

I enjoyed the concept but thought it looked a bit overwhelming and would be too much for consumers to refer back to easily. I then removed the V shape. I considered using the D I had highlighted from the vault to spell DocuVault.

I changed this and added the full name DocuVault underneath the logo. As the app is not known, I thought having the name underneath would make the logo more recognisable.
